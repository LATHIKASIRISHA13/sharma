ROHITH SHARMA

ALL ABOUT ROHITH SHARMA

EARLY LIFE

Sharma was on 30 April 1987 in Bansod, Nagpur, Maharashtra, India.[7] His mother, Purnima Sharma, is from Visakhapatnam, Andhra Pradesh.[8] His father, Gurunath Sharma, worked as a caretaker of a transport firm storehouse. Sharma was raised by his grandparents and uncles in Borivali because of his father's low income. He would visit his parents, who lived in a single-room house in Dombivli, only during weekends.[9] He has a younger brother, Vishal Sharma.[10] His mother tongue is Telugu.

PERSONAL LIFE

Sharma married his longtime girlfriend, Ritika Sajdeh on 13 December 2015 whom he first met in 2008. They have one child, a girl born on 30 December 2018 named Samaira.[12] Sharma is a practitioner of the meditation technique Sahaj Marg.

YOUTH AND DOMESTIC FIRST-CLASS CAREER

Sharma made his List A debut for West Zone against Central Zone in the Deodhar Trophy at Gwalior in March 2005. Batting at number eight, he scored 31 not out as West Zone won by 3 wickets with 24 balls remaining. Cheteshwar Pujara and Ravindra Jadeja made their debuts in the same match.[18] It was Sharma's unbeaten innings of 142 in 123 balls against North Zone at the Maharanna Bhupal College Ground in Udaipur in the same tournament that brought him into the limelight.[19] He visited Abu Dhabi and Australia with the India A squad and was then included among India's 30-member probable's list for the upcoming ICC Champions Trophy tournament, although he did not make the final squad.

INTERNATIONAL CAREER

Sharma had a successful home series against England in 2021. Instrumental in his team's comeback after a defeat in the first Test at Chennai, he scored a century, one that The Guardian termed "deserves to be considered one of the greatest this century."[39] He put on 167 runs with Ajinkya Rahane for the fourth wicket while making 161 runs in an innings that included 18 fours and two sixes. India went on to win the Test by 317 runs.[41] He top-scored for his side in both innings of the low-scoring third Test in Ahmedabad with scores of 66 and 25 contributing to his team's win.[42] Sharma finished the series aggregating 345 runs, the most for India, at an average of 58.[39] He went on to score his first overseas Test century on 4 September 2021 with an innings of 127 against England at The Oval, also reaching the milestone of 3,000 runs in Test cricket.

PLAYING STYLE

Sharma is an aggressive batsman but plays with style and elegance. He is usually an opening batsman in limited overs cricket, but has played most of his Test cricket as a middle-order batsman.[104][105] In limited overs cricket, Sharma is widely recognised as one of the format's most outstanding batsmen.[106][107] And for his attacking batting and six hitting abilities he often referred as Hitman.

ACHIEVEMENTS

Sharma clinched the world record for the highest ever individual score in a one-day international match, with a remarkable 264 against Sri Lanka at Eden Gardens, Kolkata, on 13 November 2014.[7] He is the only player to have scored three double-centuries in this form of international cricket.[111] In January 2020, Sharma was named as the ODI Player of the Year by the International Cricket Council (ICC).During the 2019 World Cup, Sharma became the only batsman to scored five centuries in a single edition of the Cricket World Cup.
